The Greater Good

Ronak Gothi
May 10, 2023
Updated on May 10, 2023

When you start doing deeds which might not benefit you in any way and can sometime not in the best interest of your own-self, you are a part of the greater-good club.

Try answering the following questions

  • Why is the tax collection in so many countries still low?
  • Why is corruption so rampant?
  • Why did some countries had and continues to have dictators?
  • Why is healthcare so unaffordable?
  • Why are women sexually assaulted?
  • Why do politicians sometime find ways to stay in power forever?
  • Why are some discovery important to humans existence patented?
  • Why do society have so much wealth inequality?
  • Why did slave-trade exist?

The answer to many of the questions above can be inversely co-related to the "Greater Good Club". We have more people who act in their self-interest, instead of thinking of the greater good.

But there exists individuals how have made significant contribution to human evolution, whilst not enriching one self [1][2][3][4][5]. Imagine the world where we have more people in the "Greater Good" Club, when compared to the "Self Love" Club? Wouldn't the world be such a lovely place to live?
